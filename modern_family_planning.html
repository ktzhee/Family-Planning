A random statistic

Did you know? Nearly 1 in 2 pregnancies in the Philippines are unplanned, highlighting the crucial need for accessible family planning resources to promote healthier families and communities.

Importance of family planning

    Protects Health and Well-being

  • Family planning allows parents to prepare physically, emotionally, and financially for each child. It reduces health risks for mothers and babies, and helps families thrive together in a safer, more stable home.

    • Promotes Economic Stability

  • Planning for the right family size ensures resources—like food, education, and healthcare—are distributed responsibly. Families can better manage expenses and work towards improved opportunities for all members.

Modern Family Planning Methods

    Barrier Methods

  • These physically block sperm from reaching the egg. Examples include male and female condoms, diaphragms, and cervical caps. They are affordable, protect against sexually transmitted infections, and are used only during sexual activity.

    • Hormonal Methods

  • These use hormones to prevent ovulation or thicken cervical mucus. Common types are birth control pills, patches, injections, vaginal rings, and implants placed under the skin. They require regular use or medical administration and offer high effectiveness.

    • Intrauterine Devices (IUDs)

  • Small T-shaped devices are inserted into the uterus by a healthcare provider. There are hormonal IUDs that release progestin and non-hormonal (copper) IUDs. IUDs prevent fertilization and can last from 3 to 10 years, providing long-term, reversible contraception.

A random statistic

Investing in family planning can reduce poverty rates by as much as 30% in communities with accessible resources.
Children born into planned, healthy families are more likely to stay in school, enjoy better nutrition, and grow up in loving, stable homes.
